BRAM STOKER AWARD – SUPERIOR ACHIEVEMENT IN A MIDDLE GRADE NOVEL
Formatted summary with age suitability

2022 – They Stole Our Hearts – Daniel Kraus (Age 11–14)
A dark and emotional story with eerie undertones.
Blends horror elements with themes of friendship and loss.
Atmospheric and unsettling, recommended for confident older middle-grade readers.

2022 – This Appearing House – Ally Malinenko (Age 10–13)
A haunted house story that slowly builds tension.
Focuses on grief, memory, and things that refuse to stay buried.
Creepy but thoughtful, suitable for readers who enjoy emotional ghost stories.

2022 – Camp Scare – Delilah S. Dawson (Age 9–12)
A summer camp turns terrifying when legends come alive.
Classic campfire horror mixed with humor and adventure.
Fast-paced, spooky, and very accessible.

2022 – The Clackity – Lora Senf (Age 10–13)
A girl enters a nightmarish world to save her aunt.
Inspired by folklore and dark fairy tales.
Intense and genuinely creepy, best for confident readers.

2022 – A Comb of Wishes – Lisa Stringfellow (Age 9–12)
A magical story rooted in Caribbean folklore.
A girl makes dangerous wishes to fix her family’s problems.
Gentle fantasy with light spooky elements.

2023 – The Nighthouse Keeper – Lora Senf (Age 11–14)
A mysterious guardian protects a house that should not exist.
Reality bends as shadows and monsters emerge.
Dark, atmospheric, and best for older middle grade readers.

2023 – Monster Camp – Sarah Henning (Age 9–12)
A camp for kids who are secretly monsters.
Explores identity and belonging with humor.
Fun, spooky, and approachable.

2023 – Los Monstruos: Felice and the Wailing Woman – Diana López (Age 9–12)
A girl confronts legendary creatures from Mexican folklore.
Culture, family, and myth intertwine.
Spooky but warm and accessible.

2023 – Frances and the Werewolves of the Black Forest – Refe Tuma (Age 10–13)
A gothic-style adventure with werewolves and secrets.
Classic horror atmosphere with playful moments.
Good for readers who enjoy suspense without extreme scares.

2023 – What Stays Buried – Suzanne Young (Age 11–14)
A story about uncovering dark truths from the past.
Horror tied to guilt and trauma.
Psychologically intense, better for older readers.

2024 – There’s Something Sinister in Center Field – Robert P. Ottone (Age 9–12)
A baseball season turns frighteningly wrong.
Sports and supernatural horror collide.
Fun, eerie, and very readable.

2024 – The Creepening of Dogwood House – Eden Royce (Age 10–13)
A Southern gothic ghost story.
History, hauntings, and family secrets collide.
Moody and slow-burn, ideal for patient readers.

2024 – The Curse of Eelgrass Bog – Mary Averling (Age 9–12)
A small town is haunted by an ancient curse.
Nature and folklore play central roles.
Suspenseful but not overwhelming.

2024 – The Witch in the Woods – Michaelbrent Collings (Age 10–13)
A survival horror story set deep in the woods.
Tension escalates quickly.
Scarier than average, best for confident readers.

2024 – The No-Brainer’s Guide to Decomposition – Adrianna Cuevas (Age 10–13)
A mix of science, horror, and dark humor.
Explores death and decay in an adventurous way.
Smart, funny, and mildly creepy.
